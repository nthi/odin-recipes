Gnocchi

Image by congerdesign on Pixabay.

Description

If you are like me, you can't get pre-packaged gnocchi in your local grocery store! The great news is that it's relatively easy to make gnocchi from scratch if you've got potatoes, egg, and flour. It takes a couple hours start to finish, but about half of that time is passive (the potatoes are baking). Recipe adapted from Natasha's Kitchen. Makes 4 servings. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes (I use 8 small). Around 2lbs if you have a scale.
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup AP flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional: couple shakes garlic powder
  • Optional: couple shakes onion powder

Steps

  1. Wash, pierce, and bake the potatoes until you can easily poke them through with a butter knife (try a preheated oven at 400 degrees Fahrenheit for 45-60 minutes).
  2. Let the potatoes cool.
  3. While the potatoes cool, mix your flour together with the salt and spices.
  4. After mixing the flour, beat your egg in a small bowl or ramekin. Set aside.
  5. Clean your counter! You're going to be using it to roll dough!
  6. Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle (but ideally still pretty warm), remove their peels.
  7. Prepare the potatoes:
    • If you have a potato ricer: cut potatoes into smaller pieces and rice them directly onto the clean counter.
    • If you don't have a ricer: use a box grater to grate the potatoes directly onto the clean counter.
    • Try and distribute your little potato piles in an even spread as you go, or gently scoot them around once you're done ricing/grating
  8. Drizzle your egg as evenly as you can over the spread out potatoes.
  9. Sprinkle your flour/spice mix evenly over everything.
  10. Mix everything together.
    • If you have a bench scraper, use it to fold the mix over itself until the wet and dry ingredients are distributed more or less evenly.
    • If you don't have a bench scraper, use your hands to fold the mix over itself until the wet and dry ingredients are distributed more or less evenly.
    • Once it looks like a crumbly sticky mess, start gently kneading it together so that it becomes less "sticky-patches-and-crumbly-patches" and more "this is mashed potato dough"
    • You can sprinkle a little flour over it if you've got super sticky trouble. Think 1 tbs or less at a time, though.
    • Hint: You will have best results not working the dough too much. Pull it together in about 3 minutes or less. If it holds together and you can roll it into cylinders, stop kneading and move on.
    • Think biscuits or dumplings, if you've made those before!
  11. Once the dough holds together, form into a ball or log and set to one side of your workspace.
  12. Cut off about 1/8 of your dough to work with. Dust the counter with flour and roll the dough into a long cylinder.
    • You want a 1/2 inch diameter (or slightly smaller) cylinder.
  13. Use your dough scraper, a rotary pizza cutter, or a knife to cut your cylinder into gnocchi. I like to cut them between 1/2 and 1 inch long.
  14. Dust your cut gnocchi with flour and scoot to the side of your workspace (or put in a large bowl or on a plate).
  15. Repeat with rest of dough.
    • I don't use anything to put ridges on my gnocchi. They're not as pretty but they cook and taste fine!
  16. Remember to keep dusting your gnocchi, especially if you are putting them in a bowl before cooking. I just toss them all each time I add more to the bowl.
  17. Boil the gnocchi: Use lots of salted water (6 quarts) in a big pot, bring to boil, add the gnocchi.
    • Have a colander or sieve-strainer And a slotted spoon ready.
    • Have a pan with your preferred sauce ready and warming (any jarred pasta sauce works), because you're going to transfer drained gnocchi right into it.
    • Boil about half the gnocchi at a time, stirring gently as it cooks. After it floats to the top of the water, cook about 1 more minute. Scoop out and drain gnocchi. Leave your boiling water alone.
    • Drop drained gnocchi right into your sauce. Alternately (maybe you want to fry the gnocchi in butter, or just toss with butter and parm into a bowl), you can set it aside in your colander until the second batch is done.
    • Repeat with second half of gnocchi.
  18. After gnocchi has boiled, finish with your pasta sauce of choice (or fry in your fat of choice and season, or toss with butter/cheese, etc.). I heat it in a pan with jarred sauce until everything is the right temp to serve.
  19. Plate/bowl and serve! Tastes really good with fresh parmesan, olive oil drizzle, herbs, red pepper, etc.
