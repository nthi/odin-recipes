Savory Oats

Description

If you are tired of sweets for breakfast, you might enjoy a savory take on classic oatmeal. Making savory oats in the microwave won't take any longer than usual, and you can tailor the recipe to your tastes. Makes one serving. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup oats (quick or old fashioned, but not steel cut)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon Better than Bouillon, any flavor (I like chicken or vegetable)
  • Optional seasoning ideas (mix, match, follow your heart)
    • One or two shakes of red pepper flakes
    • One garlic clove, chopped
    • Dried onion flakes
    • Couple shakes of black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
    • Parmesan
    • Paprika
    • Etc.

Steps

  1. Put all ingredients into microwave safe bowl and stir (to break up the bouillon).
    • Your oats are going to bubble up a bit, so make sure it's a bigger bowl.
  2. Microwave on regular/high power for 4 minutes.
    • You know your microwave best! If 4 minutes on high doesn't make sense, go with your usual oatmeal settings and keep an eye on it.
  3. Stir the oats. If they are very watery, heat for another minute at a time.
    • If they're only a little watery, just let them sit while your oats cool. They'll thicken.
  4. Add any final toppings you'd like-- giardiniera, salsa, hot sauce, more spices, melty cheese, butter, fried egg, fermented veggies, etc.
  5. If you're feeling weird about your oatmeal being savory, remember: cheese grits/polenta/rice porridge are similar and super tasty!
